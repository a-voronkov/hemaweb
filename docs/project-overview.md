# Project Overview

## Introduction

Blood donation is critical to healthcare, yet blood banks frequently face shortages. In Thailand, only a small fraction of daily blood demand is met and less than 3% of the population donates, far below WHO recommendations. Shortages are driven by low awareness, limited understanding of the donation process, and lack of convenient donation points.

HemaWeb is a web-based platform that connects hospitals and verified donors in real time. Donors can register, confirm eligibility, receive alerts about nearby donation opportunities, and learn about the donation process. Hospitals gain faster access to suitable donors, helping to close the gap between supply and demand.

## Terminology

- **Blood drives** – scheduled blood donation events created by hospitals/medical centers.
- **Medical centers** – hospitals or clinics partnered with HemaWeb.
- **Medical organization** – an umbrella medical group that manages multiple medical centers.
- **Donors** – people who donate blood.
- **Users** – donors who use the HemaWeb application.
- **Staff** – accounts for personnel of a medical center, tied to exactly one center.
- **Admin** – staff member with elevated permissions for managing a specific medical center.
- **Super admin** – account with the highest authority inside a medical organization, across its medical centers.

## Problem statements

- Blood supply often faces shortages due to a lack of donors.
- Donors struggle to find fitting donation opportunities, primarily because of low awareness.
- New donors lack knowledge and guidance on how to start donating.
- Misinformation and stigma about blood donation and its risks discourage participation.

## Objectives

- **Increase blood supply through donor engagement** – grow and retain an active donor base to reduce chronic shortages.
- **Enhance awareness and accessibility** – make it easy for donors to discover matching donation opportunities via alerts, real-time locations, and clear information.
- **Simplify onboarding of new donors** – provide trusted, beginner-friendly guidance to remove barriers for first-time donors.
- **Improve the experience for repeat donors** – support reminders, cooldown tracking, and convenient tools for regular donors.
- **Combat misinformation and stigma** – deliver accurate educational content that builds confidence in blood donation.

## How HemaWeb addresses barriers

Research (e.g. Romero-Domínguez et al., 2019; Lertpaisankul et al., 2020; Venkateshwarlu et al., 2025) identifies the main barriers to getting and retaining donors. HemaWeb’s features are designed to target these.

### Barriers to getting new donors

- **Fear and lack of information**  
  HemaWeb exposes educational content on the public-facing home page, so prospective donors can learn about blood donation before verification.

- **Absence of a personal request to donate**  
  The platform sends targeted notifications and shows urgent requests from hospitals/medical centers, turning abstract need into concrete calls to action.

- **Inconvenience of donation venues**  
  Donors can search for nearby blood drives and emergency requests using location-aware features.

### Barriers to retaining existing donors

- **Lack of information about locations/opening times**  
  Donors can browse active blood drives and connected medical centers instead of relying on ad‑hoc knowledge.

- **No reminder for next eligible donation date**  
  The app tracks cooldown periods and surfaces a visible countdown until the next eligible donation.

- **Schedule incompatibility**  
  By listing multiple concurrent blood drives and requests, donors can choose the option that fits their schedule.
