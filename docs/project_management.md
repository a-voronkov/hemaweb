# Project Management and Methodology

## Methodology

For HemaWeb, the team adopted a structured, plan-driven methodology. The rationale:

- Work through clear, sequential stages (research, requirements, design, UI prototyping, implementation, testing).
- Reduce confusion by validating each stage before moving to the next.
- Ensure compliance considerations (especially around medical data and PDPA) are integrated from the start.

### Research Phase

The team started by researching problems in the Thai medical industry related to blood donation:

- Lack of donors and chronic shortages.
- Low donor retention.
- Inconvenience and lack of clarity for donors.

Global research showed that many of these pain points persist even in developed countries.

### Requirements and Design Phase

Based on research, the team:

- Defined main requirements for donors, hospitals, and staff.
- Identified privacy and data security as major concerns.
- Emphasized convenience and clarity for donors as key design drivers.

System design used:

- **Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs)** – to show how information moves between users, systems, and data stores.
- **Entity Relationship (ER) Diagrams** – to model database structure.

### UI Prototyping

A UI prototype was prepared to:

- Visualize donor, hospital staff, admin, and system admin flows.
- Collect feedback on navigation and usability.
- Refine design before full implementation.

### Implementation and Testing (Planned)

Planned development approach:

- Build in modules: donor side, medical center side, admin side, and supporting services.
- Track progress via modular milestones.
- Conduct extensive testing to ensure reliability, correctness, and PDPA compliance.

### Final Reporting

The project will end with a final report documenting:

- The process followed.
- The system design and implementation.
- Outcomes and lessons learned.
